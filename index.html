🏀 The Crossover – Locker Room Mission

Independent 90-minute reading simulation for A2/B1 learners. Work with your printed student book pages.

90:00

Mission Briefing

You are part of the school basketball team’s support group. Josh and JB are twins, brothers and teammates. Something changes between them. Your mission is to understand the five text parts in the correct order and explain the conflict.

You need: your printed text pages, paper, a pen and this website. Do the paper tasks carefully. Your teacher can check them later.

Work rule: First read the text part in your book. Then complete the online task. Then write the paper task.

Phase 1 – Before Reading: Prediction ⏱ 8 min

Look at the titles of the five text parts: JB and I, Girls, How Do You Spell Trouble?, Bad News and the introduction about the twins.

1A Online: What might happen?

1B Paper: Prediction boxes

Draw five boxes on your paper, one for each text part. In each box, write one prediction and one question you have.

Phase 2 – Text 1: “JB and I” ⏱ 15 min

Read the first text part carefully. Your job is to create two character files.

Josh

JB

2B Evidence check

Who is a shooting guard?
Who went to summer camps?
What is JB thinking more about?

2C Paper: Twin comparison

Draw a Venn diagram: Josh / both / JB. Add at least 4 points for each twin and 2 points for both.

Phase 3 – Text 2: “Girls” ⏱ 15 min

Read the second text part. Focus on emotions and the relationship.

3A Emotion detective

Choose two feelings Josh may have and explain them with evidence.

3B Why does Josh say: “I’m the one who can dunk”?

3C Paper: Relationship thermometer

Draw a thermometer from 0 to 10. 0 = enemies, 10 = perfect brothers. Mark the relationship after Text 2 and write 3 reasons.

Phase 4 – Text 3: “How Do You Spell Trouble?” ⏱ 15 min

Read the third text part. Now the conflict becomes a school problem.

4A Put the events in the correct order

Write numbers 1–6.

JB passes / gives Josh a note.
The teacher looks directly at Josh.
Josh feels very nervous and sweaty.
Josh knows what he has to do.
A window is opened because the room feels uncomfortable.
Josh decides to protect JB / take the heat.

4B Online: Josh's thought bubble

4C Paper: Decision map

Draw two paths: “Tell the truth” and “Protect JB”. For each path, write 2 possible consequences.

Phase 5 – Text 4: “Bad News” ⏱ 15 min

Read the fourth text part. Josh talks to his mother and learns the consequences.

5A Reading comprehension

Where is Josh?
What is the good news?
What is the bad news?
What does Josh want to say?

5B Explain the moral problem

5C Paper: Evidence table

Create a table with 3 columns: Event – What Josh feels – Why it matters for the brothers. Add at least 5 rows.

Phase 6 – Final Writing: Dialogue after school ⏱ 12 min

Write a short dialogue between Josh and JB later that day. They must talk about the note, the girl, basketball and trust.

Your dialogue must include: 8–12 lines, at least 6 vocabulary words from the vocabulary chapter, one apology, one accusation, one possible solution.

Phase 7 – Creative Product ⏱ 13 min

Choose one product. You may prepare it on paper or digitally.

Option A: Storyboard

Create 6 panels: twins before conflict – JB changes – girl scene – note/test – Mom's office – future solution.

Option B: Locker Room Poster

Title: “Brothers or Rivals?” Add 4 key words, 2 quotes/short text references and one advice sentence.

Option C: Voice Message

Record Josh's 60-second message to JB. Include feelings, problem and solution.

Option D: Instagram-style Post

Create a picture idea, caption and 3 comments from Josh/JB/friend.

Planning note

Final Exit Ticket ⏱ 5 min

1. What did JB do wrong?
2. What did Josh do right and wrong?
3. What can teenagers learn from the story?
4. Which 8 words can you remember?
